About BDCE Sevagram

Yeshwant Rural Education Society (Yeshwant Grammin Sanstha) was founded with an objective to carrer to the needs of rural area population
in terms of education facilities especially in remote village of Wardha District

The concept of society was conceived by Shree Bapuraoji Deshmukh, a close associate and a true follower of Mahatma Gandhi and Ex-Member of Parliament.
He established this Society with a firm belief that Teaching is a Mission and not a Profession! in the year 1959. The Yeshwant Rural Education Society
is registered on 14-09-1959 under the society Registration Act XXI of 1860 with Registration Number E 75/59 and registered on 13-07-1961 under
Bombay Public Trust Act XXIX of 1950 at the Public Registration Office Nagpur Region, Nagpur with Registration Number F-2(W).

Objective

  • To develop linkage with industry for system improvement.
  • To create the Centre of Academic Excellence.
  • To bring industry closer to the academic and vice-versa.
  • To integrate industrial training and other inputs from the industry with the teaching learning processes.
  • To offer research, development, consultancy and testing services to solve industrial problems.
  • To create a holistic platform at BDCE for placement drive of undergraduate and postgraduate students.
  • To organize the collaborative value-based, need-based staff development program for industrial personnel and stakeholders of academia.
  • To promote entrepreneurship in technical institution.
